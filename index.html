Letter to My Friend — June, 2002

I have a story to tell you, and it’s about my wife. You see, Martha has become what my father used to call a life snatcher.

I know you’re fond of my wife, my dear friend. I know you still cherish the times we were in school and she’d cook us rice and egusi with her last dime. I know you still remember how she paid my school fees in final year when I almost dropped out. But I tell you, she has changed.

My father once warned me — on his deathbed, no less — “If you want to live into your old age, son… do not trust a woman.” I thought it was death speaking, not wisdom. But now… now I see.

Martha cheated on me, my friend. And when I confronted her, when I saw the messages… do you know what she said? She said it was you.
